# Science & Nature Starter template cleanup

Date: 2026-07-16

## Reason

The starter pack had become dominated by three mechanically generated question
families that rewarded obscure reference-table memorization more than useful
science knowledge. Some atomic-number prompts even used systematic placeholder
names for hypothetical elements. The same pattern appeared with official
three-letter constellation abbreviations, which are specialist catalog labels
rather than broadly useful astronomy knowledge.

## Actions

| Question family | Found | Action |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Element atomic numbers | 16 | Replaced all 16 |
| Constellation abbreviations | 16 | Replaced all 16 |
| Element symbols | 16 | Replaced 11 obscure examples; retained 5 familiar examples |
| **Total reviewed** | **48** | **43 replaced; 5 retained and rebalanced** |

The retained symbol questions are helium, magnesium, boron, sulfur, and nickel.
Their difficulty was reduced to levels 1 or 2 so they function as accessible
chemistry fundamentals instead of inflated challenge questions.

Replacement questions broaden the pack across astronomy, atmospheric and Earth
science, chemistry, thermodynamics, cell and molecular biology, evolution,
ecology, botany, and physics. Examples include spectroscopy, stellar evolution,
plate boundaries, entropy, chemical bonding, osmosis, ribosomes, the Doppler
effect, and the endosymbiotic theory.

## Safeguards

- No question was removed from the pack without a replacement.
- The Science & Nature Starter pack remains at 100 questions: 95 generated
  catalog questions plus 5 original seed questions.
- The generated catalog's original difficulty distribution was preserved.
- All atomic-number lookup prompts and all constellation-abbreviation prompts
  were eliminated from the starter pack.
- The replacement questions retain immutable catalog keys so production updates
  create new question versions without discarding player attempts, feedback, or
  history attached to the underlying questions.
